Executive Summary

The Opportunity

The 2026 FIFA World Cup creates a unique cultural moment to deliver a visible, lasting community sport legacy. This proposal offers government a high-impact, low-risk mechanism to reach young people in England's most deprived communities.

The Ask

A £20 million Growth Fund structured as a 50:50 public-private partnership — £10m government investment matched by £10m private philanthropy — to scale a proven early-intervention model nationally.

The Model

Hyperlocal football provision within 2km of participants' homes, using trusted adult relationships to engage young people least likely to participate in organised sport. Heavily subsidised sessions delivered in partnership with schools and community venues.

The Return

At £50 per young person (government contribution), this represents exceptional value compared to remedial interventions. Sport England's SROI methodology indicates £4.38 return for every £1 invested in community sport.

Scoring Methodology

Overview

The city selection framework uses a two-stage process: a primary filter (hard exclusion criteria) followed by a weighted scoring index to produce defensible, evidence-based decisions.

Stage 1: Primary Filter

Cities must pass ALL criteria to be considered:

  • Deprivation threshold: Child poverty rate ≥30% (Before Housing Costs) OR IDACI rank in national top 50
  • Scale threshold: Child population (ages 0-17) ≥50,000

Cities failing any criterion are automatically classified as NO-GO regardless of other factors.

Stage 2: Weighted Scoring Index

Cities passing the primary filter receive a composite score (0-100) based on five weighted factors:

Total Score = (Child Poverty Normalised × 0.30) + (IDACI Rank Normalised × 0.25) + (Obesity Rate Normalised × 0.15) + (Child Population Normalised × 0.15) + (Core City Bonus × 0.15)

Normalisation Method

Each metric is normalised to a 0-100 scale using min-max normalisation against the dataset of all English local authorities:

Normalised = ((Value - Min) / (Max - Min)) × 100

For IDACI rank (where lower = more deprived), the scale is inverted so higher scores indicate greater need.

Decision Thresholds

  • GO: Passes primary filter AND score ≥60
  • WATCH: Passes primary filter AND score 45-59
  • NO-GO: Fails primary filter OR score <45

Data Sources

  • Child poverty (BHC): End Child Poverty / Loughborough University CRSP, FYE 2024
  • IDACI: English Indices of Deprivation 2025 (IoD25), MHCLG, published November 2025
  • Childhood obesity: NCMP Year 6 data, OHID, academic year 2022/23
  • Child population: ONS Mid-Year Population Estimates, 2023
  • Core City status: Core Cities UK membership list

Validation

The framework was stress-tested to ensure it produces defensible results. Notably, Newcastle (a target city) scores 60.7 — just above the GO threshold — demonstrating the criteria were not artificially tuned to include preferred cities. Leeds and Sheffield (large Core Cities) are excluded on deprivation grounds, providing confidence the framework genuinely prioritises need over convenience.
